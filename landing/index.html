Four modes, one history

🎳 Bowl-off

Bowl your real frames and compete against a chosen rival, frame by frame. Tap the pins you knock down; the rival bowls the same breaking-down lanes.

📖 Journal

Record your real shots — what you saw, what you decided, what happened. Annotate any frame of a live game to sharpen your lane read.

🎥 Trace soon

Film a shot and auto-track the ball — laydown, breakpoint, pocket entry and speed, with a top-down path of every throw.

🏃 Form soon

Film your approach — on-device pose tracking reads your swing plane, posture and footwork, step by step. Nothing leaves your phone.

Built for league night

🧠 A real simulation engine

Rivals aren't random numbers — rev rate and ball speed drive results, oil breaks down as the game goes on, and ball changes matter.

🏆 Rivals with personality

Build a roster of opponents, each with their own style and scout report. Pick your poison before every bowl-off — or go solo for stats.

🎳 Your arsenal

Track your balls, switch mid-game with one tap, and see how each one performs across patterns and centres.

🏟️ Your centres

Save the houses you bowl at and their patterns, so every game is logged against the conditions you actually faced.

📊 Stats that matter

Average, strike %, spare and makable %, and breakdowns by pattern, ball and centre — across every mode, in one shared history.

🔒 Private by design

No accounts, no uploads, no analytics. Everything lives on your device, and you can export a backup file anytime.

Get it on your phone

📱 Install the PWA

Open the app in your phone's browser, then add it to your home screen — it opens full-screen and works offline after the first load.

Open virtuallanes.app

iPhone (Safari): Share → Add to Home Screen. Android (Chrome): menu (⋮) → Install app.

🛠️ Host it yourself

It's a static SvelteKit build — deploy it to any static host, or just run it locally.

git clone https://github.com/retroverse-studios/virtual-lanes
cd virtual-lanes
pnpm install
pnpm build   # static site in ./build

No backend, no database — the build output is the whole app.
